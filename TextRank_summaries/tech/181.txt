early next year travellers in the city of hanau, near frankfurt, will be able to pay for tickets by passing their phone over a smart card reader already installed on the buses.using a mobile phone is the next step, said gerhard romen, head of market development at nokia.the ticketless trial will start early in 2005 and people will also be able to access transport information and timetables via their phones.nokia has worked with electronics giant philips to develop a shell for the mobile phone that will be compatible with hanau's existing ticketing system.
