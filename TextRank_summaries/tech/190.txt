sony, ibm and toshiba have released limited data about the so called cell chip that will be able to carry out trillions of calculations per second.the three firms have been working on the chip since 2001 but before now few details have been released about how it might function.in a joint statement the three firms gave hints about how the chip will work but fuller details will be released in february next year at the international solid state circuits conference in san francisco.the three firms claim that the cell chip will be up to 10 times more powerful than existing processors.when put inside powerful computer servers, the cell consortium expects it to be capable of handling 16 trillion floating point operations, or calculations, every second.the chip has also been refined to be able to handle the detailed graphics common in games and the data demands of films and broadband media.
