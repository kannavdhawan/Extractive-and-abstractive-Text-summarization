a survey of wi fi networks in london, frankfurt, new york and san francisco by rsa security found more than a third had basic security features turned off.wireless or wi fi networks have become hugely popular over the last few years in offices because they are easy to set up and make going online much more convenient.this popularity shows no sign of slowing reveals the annual rsa security and netsurity survey which found an annual 66% growth rate in the number of wireless nets being installed in london and frankfurt.on average 33% of the wireless networks found by rsa and netsurity researchers in london, frankfurt, new york and san francisco had not used basic security systems.many firms were simply turning on their wireless net access points and use default settings that anyone familiar with wi fi could easily find out.rsa said that 26% of wi fi networks found london used default settings compared to 30% in frankfurt, 31% in new york and 28% in san francisco.
