eu software patent law delayed controversial new eu rules for the patenting of computer based inventions have been put on hold due to a last minute intervention from poland.poland a large and therefore crucial eu member has requested more time to consider the issue, especially as it relates to the patenting of software.critics say the law would favour large companies over small, innovative ones.polish ministers want to see the phrasing of the text of the directive on the patentability of computer implemented inventions changed so that it excludes the patenting of software.patent rights, it is claimed, protect inventors but in this case it is exclusively the interests of the big companies that are protected, eva lichtenberg, a austrian green member of the european parliament said in a statement.
