turkey's current 18.6bn loan agreement with the imf expires in february and the new deal would see it receive added support between 2005 and 2007.in return for the funding, turkey would be expected to keep inflation under control and introduce market reforms.the turkish economics minister, ali babacan, said the two sides had reached general agreement on a new three year funding program.rodrigo de rato, the imf's managing director, said the loan agreement would help to improve turkish economic prospects by cutting its debt and stimulating growth.i believe the new programme, if implemented successfully, will help turkey create the conditions for sustained growth and employment creation, reduce inflation toward european level and enhance the economy's resilience, he said.as part of the draft agreement, turkey has signed a letter of intent stating its determination to push through far reaching reforms to its tax and benefits system and its banking sector.
