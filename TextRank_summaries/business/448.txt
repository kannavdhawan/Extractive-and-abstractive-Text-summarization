us ahold suppliers face charges us prosecutors have charged nine food suppliers with helping dutch retailer ahold inflate earnings by more than 800m 428m .ahold hit the headlines in february 2003 after it emerged that there were accounting irregularities at its us subsidiary foodservice.ahold has admitted that it fraudulently inflated promotional allowances at foodservice, improperly consolidated joint ventures and also committed other accounting errors and irregularities.the nine now charged, who worked as suppliers to ahold, are accused of signing false documents relating to the amount of money they paid the retailer for promoting their products in its stores.food companies pay supermarkets and retailers for prime shelf space.the suppliers in question are said to have inflated the amount of money they paid, providing auditors with signed letters that allowed ahold to inflate its earnings.it was less severe than many had envisaged, however, and since then ahold has worked hard at rebuilding its reputation and investor confidence.
