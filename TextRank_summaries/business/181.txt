india and russia in energy talks india and russia are to work together in a series of energy deals, part of a pact which could see india invest up to 20bn in oil and gas projects.on the agenda are oil and gas extraction as well as transportation deals, to be led by russian energy giant gazprom and india's ongc.the former yukos subsidiary was controversially sold off last year and eventually acquired by state owned energy giant rosneft.russian media reported that india and russia signed a memorandum of understanding on energy co operation on tuesday during a meeting between oil and natural gas corporation chairman subir raha, gazprom chairman aleksey miller and india's petroleum minister mani shankar aiyar.ongc could invest in gas and oil fields in sakhalin, in the far east of russia, and may also take part in joint tender bids for projects in eastern siberia and the caspian sea.ongc's mr raha said the two could work together on joint bids from next year.russian news agencies reported that india's petroleum minister mr aiyar and russian energy minister viktor khristenko would discuss the future of yugansk at a meeting on tuesday.however, he stressed that ongc was not interested in a 'loan for oil deal' in connection to yugansk, similar to that concluded recently between rosneft and china's national petroleum corporation.
