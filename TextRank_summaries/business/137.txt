last month, bank of england governor mervyn king said that the economy had hit a softer patch after rapid economic growth in the first half of 2004.richard jeffrey, chief economist at bridgewell securities, said it was very unlikely that the bank of england would put rates up again this time around.there have been sufficient signs in the economy of a slowdown to stay the bank of england's hand, he told bbc radio 4's today programme.however, mr jeffrey said he believed the slowdown in economic activity was temporary and it was dangerous to assume that rates had peaked.
