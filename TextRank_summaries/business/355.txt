israeli economy picking up pace israel's economy is forecast to grow by 4.2% in 2004 as it continues to emerge from a three year recession.the economy is benefiting from a quieter period in palestinian israeli violence and a pick up in global demand for technology products.the growth figures come about despite a strike earlier this year by about 400,000 public sector worker which closed banks, hospitals, postal services and transport facilities.imports gained by 13%, signalling that domestic demand has picked up again.
