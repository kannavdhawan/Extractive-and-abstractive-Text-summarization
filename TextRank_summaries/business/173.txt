brewers' profits lose their fizz heineken and carlsberg, two of the world's largest brewers, have reported falling profits after beer sales in western europe fell flat.danish brewer carlsberg suffered a 3% fall in profits due to waning demand and increased marketing costs.it had warned in september that the weakening us dollar, which has cut the value of foreign sales, would knock 125m euros off its operating profits.despite the dip in profits, heineken's sales have been improving and total revenue for the year was 10bn euros, up 8.1% from 9.26bn euros in 2003.heineken said it now plans to invest 100m euros in aggressive and high impact marketing in europe and the us in 2005.heineken increased its dividend payment by 25% to 40 euro cents, but warned that the continued impact of a weaker dollar and an increased marketing spend may lead to a drop in 2005 net profit.carlsberg, the world's fifth largest brewer, saw annual pre tax profits fall to 3.4bn danish kroner 456m euros .
