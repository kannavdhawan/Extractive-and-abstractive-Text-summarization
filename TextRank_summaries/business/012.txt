indonesians face fuel price rise indonesia's government has confirmed it is considering raising fuel prices by as much as 30%.president susilo bambang yudhoyono's government has said it wants to curb fuel subsidies and direct the money into aid programmes for the poor.since president yudhoyono's government came to power in october, it has indicated its intention of raising domestic fuel prices by cutting subsidies.the january to march quarter of this year is the best time for us to increase fuel prices, said sri mulyani indrawati, state minister for national development planning.indonesia's previous government, led by president megawati sukarnoputri, also attempted to cut subsidies in 2003, but was forced to back down in the face of public protests.
