the latest scandal at bank of china will do nothing to reassure foreign investors that china's big four banks are ready for international listings.government policy sees the bank listings as vital economic reforms.however, a report in the china published economic observer said on monday that the two banks may have scrapped plans to list in new york because of the cost of meeting regulatory requirements imposed since the enron scandal.attempts to strengthen internal controls and tighten lending policies have uncovered a succession of scandals involving embezzlement by bank officials and loans for favours.the most high profile case involved the ex president of bank of china, wang xuebing, jailed for 12 years in 2003.although, he committed the offences whilst running bank of china in new york, mr wang was head of china construction bank when the scandal broke.earlier this month, a china construction bank branch manager was jailed for life in a separate case.last year, china's premier, wen jiabao, targeted bank lending practices in a series of speeches, and regulators ordered all big loans to be scrutinised, in an attempt to cool down irresponsible lending.china's leaders see reforming the top four banks as vital to distribute capital to profitable companies and protect the health of china's economic boom.
