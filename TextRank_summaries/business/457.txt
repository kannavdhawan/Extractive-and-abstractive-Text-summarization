mr schroeder said existing fiscal rules should be loosened to allow countries to run deficits above the current 3% limit if they met certain criteria.the current eu rules limit the size of a eurozone country's deficit to 3% of gdp.mr schroeder a staunch supporter of the pact when it was set up in the 1990s said exemptions were now needed to take into account the cost of domestic reform programmes and changing economic conditions.the proposed changes would make it harder for the european commission to launch infringement action against any state which breaches the pact's rules.mr schroeder's intervention comes ahead of a meeting of the 12 eurozone finance ministers on monday to discuss the pact.a number of eu finance ministers are believed to favour only limited changes to the eurozone's rules.
