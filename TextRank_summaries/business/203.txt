'standoff' on deutsche's lse bid deutsche boerse investors unhappy with its london stock exchange bid will have no chance to throw out the exchange's management until may, reuters says.the sunday times reported that hedge funds tci and atticus were planning to demand the removal of the group's chairman and chief executive.however, deutsche boerse told reuters tci's request for the removal of the supervisory board will be considered at the annual general meeting on may 25.the group owns more than 5% of deutsche more than enough to demand an extraordinary general meeting to call on shareholders to oust the german exchange's management.tci and atticus have opposed the lse bid for some time saying it would destroy shareholder value, and would be better spent on a share buyback.
