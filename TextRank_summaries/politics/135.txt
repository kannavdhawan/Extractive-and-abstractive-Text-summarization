blair says uk tsunami aid to soar tony blair has predicted the british government will eventually give hundreds of millions of pounds in aid to countries hit by the tsunami.the prime minister was speaking publicly for the first time since returning from his holiday in egypt.asked about criticism that he did not cut short his holiday, mr blair told bbc radio 4's today programme i don't think this is a situation in which the british people need me to articulate what they feel.the important thing was action and not words, he said, adding that of the 50m given by the government so far only six or seven million had been spent.my estimate is we will need to spend from government several hundred million pounds.so we will far and away more than match the generosity of the british people, he said.chancellor gordon brown earlier backed a plan to freeze the foreign debts of all the affected nations.the chancellor said the plan would initially save the most affected countries about 3bn 1.58bn in repayments.foreign secretary jack straw is to visit thailand and indonesia this week.international development secretary hilary benn will also visit aceh in indonesia, as well as sri lanka.the british public has now pledged 76m in aid with emergency supplies from the british government starting to arrive in the region on three raf flights in a joint operation with scandinavian countries.
