pm apology over jailings tony blair has apologised to two families who suffered one of the uk's biggest miscarriages of justice.the prime minister was commenting on the wrongful jailing of 11 people for ira bomb attacks on pubs in guildford and woolwich in 1974.he made the apology to members of the conlon and maguire families in his private room at westminster.however, one of the so called guildford four, gerry conlon who was wrongly convicted of planting the bombs said the families were delighted with the apology.the move followed a huge campaign in ireland for a public apology after eleven people were wrongly convicted of making and planting the ira bombs which killed seven people.mr blair's official spokesman said no one present at the meeting would ever forget the strength of feeling of relief that the prime minister's statement brought to them .all were arrested because of a family connection to gerry conlon.mr conlon's father giuseppe was arrested when travelling to london from belfast to help his son.also arrested were anne maguire and members of her family.
