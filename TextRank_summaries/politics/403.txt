voters 'don't trust politicians' eight out of 10 voters do not trust politicians to tell the truth, a new poll conducted for the bbc suggests.the poll comes after foreign secretary jack straw predicted trust would be the key choice at the next election.both the tories and the lib dems are keen to emphasise a perceived lack of trust in tony blair, following his claims over iraqi weapons.but according to the bbc poll, 61% said the issue of trust made no difference to whether or not they would vote at the next election, widely expected on 5 may.the lib dems are also keen to highlight the trust issue, with charles kennedy has claiming voters had a fundamental lack of trust in the prime minister .former education secretary estelle morris told bbc news 24 that there was a real problem of trust between the public and the politicians.she said she did not feel her own colleagues could be trusted, but suggested the three cornered relationship between the press, politicians and the public had a hand in the issue.lib dem foreign affairs spokesman menzies campbell said the iraq war had hit trust in politicians hard.
