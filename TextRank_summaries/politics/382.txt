here's a guide to the plan people coming to live and work in britain from outside the eu.if they plan to stay six months or more and are from a country with lots of tb, they would have to have a chest x ray and further tests if appropriate.all people from outside the european union who want to stay a year or more will have to undergo a full medical.people would have to prove they have an acceptable standard of health and are unlikely to be a danger to public health in the uk, or impose significant costs or demands on the nhs.people coming to britain for less than six months would not be medically tested unless they intended to work in health care, childcare or teaching.under 16s would not face tests for hepatitis and hiv.the tories say people fleeing persecution will not be denied sanctuary in britain because of poor health.however, they will undergo health checks to ensure they receive the right medical treatment and do not spread infectious diseases.they also believe there should be stricter controls over who comes into britain to ensure they are not a public health risk.applicants will be tested in their home country.home office minister des browne says the government already routinely checks people for tb if they come into the uk for six months or more from high risk countries.
