franz ferdinand's art school lesson scottish rock band franz ferdinand, who shot to prominence in 2004, have won two brit awards.with their self titled debut, franz ferdinand have achieved what most rock bands crave high credibility and critical acclaim while also selling stacks of cds.they have risen to the head of the uk's art rock ranks with an album of songs that are catchy, creative and original enough to sound fresh.in september it was named album of the year winning the prestigious mercury music prize.nevertheless franz ferdinand were the only uk band to win an mtv video music award this year, taking the breakthrough video crown for take me out.the four piece met two years ago when drummer paul thomson was working at the glasgow art school where bassist bob hardy was studying.at the turn of the year, kapranos told bbc news online the band were fed up with serious bands in that post rock thing that seemed to be doing its damndest to avoid any bloody tune .franz ferdinand had a question they asked themselves every time they wrote a song, kapranos said.the band started out with a diy ethic that saw them take over a disused art deco warehouse in glasgow and rename it the chateau.the buzz about the band soon spread around the music industry and 40 record labels turned up to one gig in glasgow which the band thought was totally ridiculous .
