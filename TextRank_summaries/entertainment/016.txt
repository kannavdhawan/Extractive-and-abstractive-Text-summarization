arthur hailey king of the bestsellers novelist arthur hailey, who has died at the age of 84, was known for his bestselling page turners exploring the inner workings of various industries, from the hotels to high finance.born in luton, bedfordshire, on 5 april 1920, hailey was the only child of working class parents, they could not afford to keep him in school beyond the age of 14.it was an occupation that was later to feature in his authorial debut, the television screenplay flight into danger.he wanted to be a writer from an early age, but did not take it up professionally until his mid thirties, when he was inspired to write his first screenplay while on a return flight to toronto.could i fly the airplane i was a rusty wartime pilot who hadn't flown for nine years.hailey worked as a screenwriter for a couple of years, before turning to novels.flight into danger was adapted to become hailey's first novel, runaway zero eight in 1958.the hit novel took four years to write, and stayed on national best seller lists for a full year.it was turned into a movie in 1967 and later adapted into a glossy soap in the 1980s, starring james brolin.airport 1968 arguably remains hailey's best loved work and prompted the disaster movie genre.the book was adapted into a hit film in 1970, starring burt lancaster as the harassed aiport manager and dean martin as a womanising pilot, alongside jean seberg and jacqueline bisset.the writer was known for his painstaking research into the professions around which each novel was centred, drawing his characters from real life and taking up to three years to produce each book.the 1997 novel detective proved to be hailey's final book, when at the age of 77 he decided to retire.his greatest ambition was to see his name on a book and he certainly achieved that, said his wife, sheila.
