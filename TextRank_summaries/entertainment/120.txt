' to mark the 60th anniversary of the birth of reggae star bob marley, rob partridge marley's former head of press at island records remembers the man behind the legend.partridge worked with marley from 1977 until the jamaican musician's death in 1981.i joined island records in 1977 and the first week i was there i worked on his show at the rainbow theatre.i remember going to see dates in milan and turin and they were enormous concerts.bob was one of the most mesmeric people i've ever had the privilege to work with.i saw him many times.i recall in 1978 he came to the uk for top of the pops and a daily mirror journalist did a half hour interview.he came back into the dressing room to resume the interview but saw a world cup match on tv.the last time i saw him was in london in 1980.bob was a good player.we played in brazil against some world cup winning players.but bob in the 1990s became one of the great icons in america and the third world.in 1991, ten years after his death, he sold more records than at any time during his life.we saw black america taking bob into their hearts for the first time.bob, in worldwide terms, is the greatest music star there has ever been.well 1981 was to be the year he toured africa with stevie wonder.of course the 1981 tour never happened, but the whole of africa would have embraced him.bob was endlessly optimistic about the way africa would turn out.
