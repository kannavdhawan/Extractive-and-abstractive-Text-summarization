bekele sets sights on world mark olympic 10,000m champion kenenisa bekele is determined to add the world indoor two mile record at february's norwich union grand prix in birmingham. compatriots mulugeta wondimu, abiyote abate and markos geneti, the world indoor bronze medallist over 3000m, will race against bekele on 18 february. the 22 year old will again be chasing a record held by his compatriot and mentor haile gebrselassie, who set the mark at the same meeting in 2003. i am still as hungry to do as much as i can in this sport, said bekele. the ethiopian broke the world indoor 5,000m record on his debut at the meeting last year. while men's 4x100m relay gold medallists jason gardener and mark lewis francis will go head to head in the 60m. gebrselassie's current record stands at eight minutes, 04.69 seconds.
