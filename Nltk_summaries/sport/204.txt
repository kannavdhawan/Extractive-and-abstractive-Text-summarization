uefa approves fake grass uefa says it will allow european matches to be played on artificial pitches from the start of next season. uefa spokesman rob faulkner said people in england have bad memories of the artificial pitches of luton and qpr in the 1980s but the latest generation are completely different and are much more like grass. several leading clubs from scandinavia russia and eastern europe especially those who only play champions league or uefa cup matches in winter are now expected to instal artificial pitches. luton preston queens park rangers and other clubs used to have plastic pitches during the the 1980s but after a two year study uefa insists the surfaces have moved on. uefa explained that the move follows comprehensive studies into the sporting and medical aspects of using artificial playing surfaces.
