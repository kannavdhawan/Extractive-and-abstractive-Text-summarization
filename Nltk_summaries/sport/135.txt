israeli club look to africa four african players, including zimbabwe goalkeeper energy murambadoro, are all ready to play for israeli club hapoel bnei sakhnin in the uefa cup. warriors' goalkeeper murambadoro, who made a name for himself at the african nations cup finals in tunisia, helped bnei sakhnin overcome albania's partizani tirana 6 1 in the previous round. the three other africans at the club are former cameroon defender ernest etchi, dr congo's alain masudi and nigerian midfielder edith agoye, who had a stint with tunisian side esperance. bnei sakhnin are the first arab side ever to play in european competition and will play english premiership side newcastle united in the first round. the club won the israeli cup final last season and are based in sakhnin, which is near haifa.
