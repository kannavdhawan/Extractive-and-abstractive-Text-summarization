although the percentage of britons without home access will have fallen slightly those that remain digital refuseniks will miss out on more the report suggests. the alliance for digital inclusion an independent body with members drawn from government industry and the voluntary sector has recently been set up to tackle some of the issues faced by the digital refuseniks. as more and more everyday tasks move online and offline services become less comprehensive the divide will become more obvious and more burdensome for those that have not got net access it predicts. around 23 million britons will miss out on a wide range of essential services such as education and medical information predicts the report by telecoms giant bt. optimists who predict that convergence and the emergence of more user friendly technology will bridge the digital divide could be way off mark the report suggests.
