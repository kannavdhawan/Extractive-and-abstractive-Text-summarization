online commons to spark debate online communities set up by the uk government could encourage public debate and build trust says the institute of public policy research ippr . with 53 of uk households now with access to the net the government suggests mr davies could act as an intermediary or middleman to set up public online places of debate and exchange to encourage more cosmopolitan politics and public trust in policy. in its report is online community a policy tool the ippr also asks if id cards could help create safer online communities. although the net is becoming part of local and central government its potential has not yet been fully exploited to create an online commons for public debate. but he adds government could play a bigger role in setting up systems of trust for online communities too.
