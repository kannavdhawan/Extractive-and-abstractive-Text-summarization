despite the good news about growth in broadband, figures from telecommunications regulator ofcom show that bt faces increasing competition, and dwindling influence, in other sectors. local loop unbundling, llu , in which bt rivals install their hardware in exchanges and take over the line to a customer's home or office, is growing steadily. this breakthrough led to a dramatic increase in orders as we were suddenly able to satisfy the pent up demand that existed in many areas, said paul reynolds, chief executive of bt wholesale which provides phone lines that other firms re sell. according to figures gathered by industry watchdog, ofcom, the growth means that the uk has now surpassed germany in terms of broadband users per 100 people. bt retail, which sells net services under its own name, also had a good quarter and provided about 30% of the new broadband customers.
