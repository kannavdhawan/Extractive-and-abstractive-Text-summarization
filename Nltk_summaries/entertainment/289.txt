musicians to tackle us red tape musicians groups are to tackle us visa regulations which are blamed for hindering british acts chances of succeeding across the atlantic. the musicians union stance is being endorsed by the music managers forum mmf who say british artists face an uphill struggle to succeed in the us thanks to the tough visa requirements which are also seen as impractical. nigel mccune from the musicians union said british musicians are disadvantaged compared to their us counterparts. a us embassy spokesman said we are aware that entertainers require visas for time specific visas and are doing everything we can to process those applications speedily. a department for media culture and sport spokeswoman said we re aware that people are experiencing problems and are working with the us embassy and record industry to see what we can do about it.
