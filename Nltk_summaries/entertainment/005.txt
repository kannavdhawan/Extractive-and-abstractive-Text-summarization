poppins musical gets flying start the stage adaptation of children's film mary poppins has had its opening night in london's west end. a number of celebrities turned out for the musical's premiere, including actress barbara windsor, comic graham norton and sir richard attenborough. sir cameron mackintosh's lavish production, which has cost 9m to bring to the stage, was given a 10 minute standing ovation. mary poppins was originally created by author pamela travers, who is said to have cried when she saw disney's 1964 film starring julie andrews. the show's director richard eyre issued a warning earlier in the week that the show was unsuitable for children under seven, while under threes are barred.
