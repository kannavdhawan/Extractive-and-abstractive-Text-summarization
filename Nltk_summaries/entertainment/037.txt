film row over pirates 'cannibals' plans to portray dominica's carib indians as cannibals in the sequel to hit film pirates of the caribbean have been criticised by the group's chief. carib chief charles williams said talks with disney's producers revealed there was a strong element of cannibalism in the script which cannot be removed . mr williams said he had received support from indigenous groups around the world in his efforts to have cannibalism references removed from the film. the caribbean island's government said disney planned to film in dominica. about 3,000 caribs live on the island of dominica, which has a population of 70,000. many caribs were killed by disease and war during colonisation up to the 1600s.
