lloyd s of london head chides fsa the head of lloyd s of london the insurance market has criticised britain s financial watchdog the financial services authority fsa . the fsa took over regulation of the general insurance sector in january but it sidestepped calls to require brokers to disclose the commissions they earn from insurers to their clients. the world s biggest insurance broker marsh mclennan said last week it would pay 850m to settle charges raised by new york attorney general eliot spitzer in october that it sought to rig bids in conjunction with insurers. the probe centred around so called contingent commissions whereby brokers were rewarded according to how much business they brought to an insurer an arrangement that did not always benefit brokers customers. in a speech on monday mr prettejohn urged the fsa to force brokers to disclose the size of their commissions.
