steel firm 'to cut' 45,000 jobs mittal steel, one of the world's largest steel producers, could cut up to 45,000 jobs over the next five years, its chief executive has said. mr mittal told us investors that once the acquisition of international steel group was completed, the company would aim to reduce its workforce by between 7,000 and 8,000 annually. it employs 50,000 staff in kazakhstan alone, and has large operations in romania, the czech republic, south africa and the united states. we are trying to create a sustainable steel industry and if we want to do that, we have to invest in new technology, a spokesman said. this could see its workforce trimmed from 155,000 to 110,000 staff by 2010. we are investing in modernisation so employees will go down, mr mittal told the conference in chicago.
