russian media reported that india and russia signed a memorandum of understanding on energy co operation on tuesday during a meeting between oil and natural gas corporation chairman subir raha, gazprom chairman aleksey miller and india's petroleum minister mani shankar aiyar. india and russia in energy talks india and russia are to work together in a series of energy deals, part of a pact which could see india invest up to 20bn in oil and gas projects. the agreement is likely to see the two companies develop refining facilities in russia, india and elsewhere and organise delivery of oil, gas and petrochemicals from russia to india and other countries across asia. ongc could invest in gas and oil fields in sakhalin, in the far east of russia, and may also take part in joint tender bids for projects in eastern siberia and the caspian sea. on the agenda are oil and gas extraction as well as transportation deals, to be led by russian energy giant gazprom and india's ongc.
