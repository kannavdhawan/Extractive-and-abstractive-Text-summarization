kraft's new advertising policy, which covers advertising on tv, radio and in print publications, is aimed at children between the ages of six and 11. it means commercials for some of its most famous snacks and cereals shown during early morning cartoon shows on tv will now be replaced by food and drink qualifying for kraft's new sensible solution label. kraft cuts snack ads for children kraft plans to cut back on advertising of products like oreo cookies and sugary kool aid drinks as part of an effort to promote healthy eating. we're working on ways to encourage both adults and children to eat wisely by selecting more nutritionally balanced diets, said lance friedmann, kraft senior vice president. the largest us food maker will also add a label to its more nutritional and low fat brands to promote the benefits. the moves come as the firms face criticism from consumer groups concerned at rising levels of obesity in us children.
