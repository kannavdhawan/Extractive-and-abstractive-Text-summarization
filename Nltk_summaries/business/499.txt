china aviation seeks rescue deal scandal hit jet fuel supplier china aviation oil has offered to repay its creditors 220m 117m of the 550m it lost on trading in oil futures. the trading scandal is the biggest to hit singapore since the 1.2bn collapse of barings bank in 1995. chen jiulin chief executive of china aviation oil cao was arrested by at changi airport by singapore police on 8 december. among the creditors whose backing cao needs for its restructuring plan are banking giants such as barclay s capital and sumitomo mitsui as well as south korean firm sk energy. the rest would come from its parent company china aviation oil holding company in beijing. of the immediate payment the firm china s biggest jet fuel supplier said it would be paying 30m out of its own resources.
