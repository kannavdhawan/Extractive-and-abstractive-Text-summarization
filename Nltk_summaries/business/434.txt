asia quake increases poverty risk nearly two million people across asia could be thrown into poverty because of the indian ocean tsunami the asian development bank adb has said. the adb fears that 1 million indonesians could join them while in india just over half a million people 645 000 are at risk of falling into poverty. sri lanka and the maldives are the two countries the adb fears are most at risk of suffering lasting economic damage from the tsunami. but the economies of the affected countries except sri lanka and the maldives should emerge with minimal damage the adb report said. mr ali added his voice to those warning that aid pledges must be promptly delivered saying the number of people at risk of poverty hinged on concerns over sanitation and health conditions and other basic needs being properly and quickly addressed .
