saudi investor picks up the savoy london's famous savoy hotel has been sold to a group combining saudi billionaire investor prince alwaleed bin talal and a unit of hbos bank. he also has substantial stakes in fairmont hotels resorts, which will manage the savoy and simpson's in the strand, and four seasons. the seller irish based property firm quinlan private bought the savoy along with the berkeley, claridge's and the connaught for 750m last year. financial details of the deal, which includes the nearby simpson's in the strand restaurant, were not disclosed. fairmont said it planned to invest 48m 26m in renovating parts of the savoy including the river room and suites with views over the river thames. prince alwaleed's hotel investments include the luxury george v in paris.
