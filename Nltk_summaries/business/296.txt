millions to lose textile jobs millions of the world s poorest textile trade workers will lose their jobs under new trade rules to be introduced in the new year a charity has warned. the losers in this new trade landscape will be some of the most vulnerable workers in countries such as bangladesh cambodia sri lanka and nepal andrew pendleton christian aid s head of trade policy said. the wto said that many developing countries support the end of quotas and stressed that funding was available to countries such as bangladesh to help them make the transition to a fully liberalised market. some of the world s fastest developing countries which rely on textile exports to build growth for example in bangladesh textiles account for almost 85 of the country s exports and the industry employs around 1.5 million people. it added that with few employment alternatives available many sacked garment workers could end up in far worse jobs with some of the mainly female workers forced into the sex trade.
