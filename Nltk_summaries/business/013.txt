in january, mitsubishi motors issued its third profits warning in nine months, and cut its sales forecasts for the year to march 2005. its sales have slid 41% in the past year, catalysed by the revelation that the company had systematically been hiding records of faults and then secretly repairing vehicles. mitsubishi motors has forecast a net loss of 472bn yen in its current financial year to march 2005. last month, it signed a production agreement with japanese rival nissan motor to supply it with 36,000 small cars for sale in japan.
