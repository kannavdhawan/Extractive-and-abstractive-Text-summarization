israeli economy picking up pace israel s economy is forecast to grow by 4.2 in 2004 as it continues to emerge from a three year recession. the growth figures come about despite a strike earlier this year by about 400 000 public sector worker which closed banks hospitals postal services and transport facilities. the economy is benefiting from a quieter period in palestinian israeli violence and a pick up in global demand for technology products. the main driver of the faster than expected expansion has been exports with tourism seeing a strong rebound the statistical office said. many companies have focused on cost cutting and greater efficiency while the government has been trying to trim public spending and push through reforms.
