european losses hit gm's profits general motors gm saw its net profits fall 37% in the last quarter of 2004, as it continued to be hit by losses at its european operations. gm reported solid overall results in 2004, despite challenging competitive conditions in many markets around the globe, gm chairman and chief executive rick wagoner said in a statement. for the whole of 2004, gm earned 3.7bn, down from 3.8bn in 2003, while its annual revenue rose 4.5% to 193bn. the us giant earned 630m 481.5m in the october to december period, down from 1bn in the fourth quarter of 2003. gm's revenues rose 4.7% to 51.2bn from 48.8bn a year earlier. excluding special items, gm's global income from continuing operations totalled 569m during the quarter, down from 838m a year earlier. the fourth quarter losses at general motors europe totalled 345m, up from 66m during the same period in 2003. gm's main european brands are opel and vauxhall.
