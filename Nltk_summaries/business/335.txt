boeing secures giant japan order boeing is to supply japan airlines with up to 50 of its forthcoming 7e7 planes in a deal that could be worth as much as 6bn 3.1bn for the us giant. the first 7e7s will be delivered to japan airlines in april 2008. boeing has set itself a target of getting 200 firm commitments for the 7e7 by the end of this year and has orders for 56 so far. asia s biggest airline joins japanese rival all nippon as one of the first carriers to order the mid size 7e7 which boeing says is super economical. boeing continues to enjoy a dominance over airbus in japan and japanese companies are taking key roles in building the 7e7. we chose the 7e7 after carefully considering both it and airbus aircraft said a japan airlines spokesman.
