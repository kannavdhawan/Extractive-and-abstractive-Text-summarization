after years of a sagging stock price and a seven year hiatus from the ftse 100, one of britain's venerable manufacturers has returned to the vaunted index, forbes said. tate lyle boss bags top award tate lyle's chief executive has been named european businessman of the year by a leading business magazine. iain ferguson was awarded the title by us publication forbes for returning one of the uk's venerable manufacturers to the country's top 100 companies. tate lyle, which was an original member of the historic ft 30 index in 1935, operates more than 41 factories and 20 more additional production facilities in 28 countries. tate's shares have leapt 55% this year, boosted by firming sugar prices and sales of its artificial sweeteners. previous winners of the forbes award include royal bank of scotland chief executive fred goodwin and former vodafone boss chris gent.
