house prices drop as sales slow house prices fell further in november and property sale times lengthened as rate rises took their toll, the royal institute of chartered surveyors found. the slowdown occurring in the market has given buyers more power to negotiate, but this time of year is traditionally a quiet one, rics housing spokesman ian perry said. in scotland, where prices have remained on an upward path, increases were more moderate , rics added. around the uk, the midlands and south saw the biggest price falls, while london prices fell but at less than the national rate. on monday, the council of mortgage lenders, british bankers association and building societies association all said mortgage lending was slowing. looking ahead, the group is anticipating a quiet start to 2005 with the market picking up in the second half prompting a 3% rise in prices over the coming 12 months.
