us company admits benin bribery a us defence and telecommunications company has agreed to pay 28.5m after admitting bribery in the west african state of benin. it agreed to pay 13m in criminal penalties as well as 15.5m to settle a civil lawsuit brought by the us financial watchdog the securities and exchange commission sec . prosecuting attorney carol lam said all us companies should take note that attempting to bribe foreign officials is criminal conduct and will be appropriately prosecuted. the sec had accused titan of illegally paying 2.1m to an unnamed agent in benin claiming ties with president kerekou. shortly after the poll which mr kerekou won benin officials agreed to quadruple titan s management fee.
