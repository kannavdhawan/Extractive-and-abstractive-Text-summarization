while the uk's jobless rate rose to 4.7% from 4.6% in the previous quarter, the rate still remains one of the lowest in the world, compared with 12.1% in germany, 10.4% in spain and 9.7% in france. but, despite more people being in work, the manufacturing sector continued to suffer, with 104,000 workers axed during the last quarter of 2004 pushing employment in the sector to a record low of 3.24 million by the end of last year. throughout 2004, the number of people in work increased by 296,000 to 28.52 million the highest figure since records began in 1971. the apparent discrepancy between rising unemployment and record numbers in work can be explained by an increase in the working population and a fall in those who are economically inactive. further rise in uk jobless total the uk's jobless total rose for the second month in a row in december, official figures show. average earnings rose by 4.3% in the year to december up from november's 4.2%, the office for national statistics ons added.
