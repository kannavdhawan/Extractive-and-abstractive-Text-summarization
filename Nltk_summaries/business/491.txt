indonesia declines debt freeze indonesia no longer needs the debt freeze offered by the paris club group of creditors economics minister aburizal bakrie has reportedly said. nevertheless one of mr bakrie s deputies mahendra siregar told afp news agency that indonesia was still considering the offer by the paris club of rich creditor nations to temporarily suspend its debt payments. indonesia which originally accepted the debt moratorium offer owes the paris club about 48bn 25.5bn . mr bakrie told the bisnis indonesia newspaper that a 1.7bn donors aid package meant that the debt moratorium was unnecessary. thailand and india have however declined the offer with thailand prefering to keep up with its payments while india said it would prefer to rely on its own resources rather than on international aid.
