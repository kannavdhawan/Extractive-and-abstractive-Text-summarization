quiksilver moves for rossignol shares of skis rossignol, the world's largest ski maker, have jumped as much as 15% on speculation that it will be bought by us surfwear firm quiksilver. the boix vives family is thought to have spent the past couple of years sounding out possible suitors for rossignol, which also makes golf equipment, snowboards and sports clothing. european sporting goods companies have seen foreign revenues squeezed by a slump in the value of the us dollar, making a takeover more attractive, analysts said. the owners of rossignol, the boix vives family, are said to be considering an offer from quiksilver. analysts believe other sporting goods companies may now take a closer look at rossignol, prompting an auction and pushing the sale price higher. rossignol shares touched 17.70 euros, before falling back to trade 7.8% higher at 16.60 euros.
