for all its faults corporate russia has become far more orderly and law abiding since 2000. companies have rushed to list their shares on international stock exchanges something unthinkable in the wilder days of the 1990s and most large firms now produce their accounts to international standards. according to the authors although a variety of changes were sweeping through russian industry at the time the increase in tax enforcement is the only likely explanation for the change of fortunes at sibneft and many of its peers. an increase in tax enforcement can increase the amount of dividends and other income outside shareholders will receive even accounting for increased levels of taxation the authors say. the calculations demonstrated what many russian analysts already knew that increasing the tax rate increases the amount that managers steal since undeclared income becomes relatively more valuable. but it argues that more rigorous tax policing has benefited all russian firms even targets of the tax police.
