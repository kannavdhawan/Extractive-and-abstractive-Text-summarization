japanese growth grinds to a halt growth in japan evaporated in the three months to september sparking renewed concern about an economy not long out of a decade long trough. but high oil prices have knocked 0.2 off the growth rate while the falling dollar means products shipped to the us are becoming relatively more expensive. the first quarter showed annual growth of 6.3 with the second showing 1.1 and economists had been predicting as much as 2 this time around. the government may now find it more difficult to raise taxes a policy it will have to implement when the economy picks up to help deal with japan s massive public debt. exports the usual engine of recovery faltered while domestic demand stayed subdued and corporate investment also fell short.
