vodafone appoints new japan boss vodafone has drafted in its uk chief executive william morrow to take charge of its troubled japanese operation. while it has more than 15 million customers in japan vodafone has found it difficult to satisfy japan s technologically demanding mobile users. vodafone is going to need to put a lot of money into japan if it wants to rebuild the business tetsuro tsusaka a telecoms analyst with deutsche bank told reuters. mr morrow will succeed shiro tsuda as president of vodafone kk japan s number three mobile operator in april. mr tsuda who will become chairman was appointed president only two months ago but the business has struggled since then losing customers in january.
