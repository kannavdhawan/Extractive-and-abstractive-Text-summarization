cars pull down us retail figures us retail sales fell 0.3 in january the biggest monthly decline since last august driven down by a heavy fall in car sales. sales at appliance and electronic stores were down 0.6 in january while sales at hardware stores dropped by 0.3 and furniture store sales dipped 0.1 . sales at clothing and clothing accessory stores jumped 1.8 while sales at general merchandise stores a category that includes department stores rose by 0.9 . the 3.3 fall in car sales had been expected coming after december s 4 rise in car sales fuelled by generous pre christmas special offers. us retail spending is expected to rise in 2005 but not as quickly as in 2004. steve gallagher us chief economist at sg corporate investment banking said january s figures were decent numbers .
