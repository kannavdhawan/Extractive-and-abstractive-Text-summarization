us firm pulls out of iraq a us company has pulled out of a major contract to rebuild iraq's transport system after attacks on reconstruction efforts, pentagon officials have said. members of the joint venture including contrack are committed to the ongoing reconstruction efforts, are actively working in iraq and continue to look for new construction opportunities in the country, it said in a statement. contrack international, of arlington, virginia, heads a coalition of firms working on a series of schemes. lt col eric schnaible of the pco told the associated press news agency contrack's withdrawal from the transportation contract was a mutually agreed to separation and did not indicate a movement by us companies to leave iraq. us firms and their workers have been targets of attacks, and security concerns are said to be a major reason for the slow pace of reconstruction in iraq. the pentagon's project and contract office pco in baghdad said it had taken over contrack's management of the subcontractors working on the transportation projects.
