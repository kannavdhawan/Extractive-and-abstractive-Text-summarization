rescue hope for borussia dortmund shares in struggling german football club borussia dortmund slipped on monday despite the club agreeing a rescue plan with creditors on friday. creditors agreed on friday to suspend interest payments until 2007. news of the deal had boosted shares in the club on friday, but the stock slipped back 7% during monday morning. the club, which has posted record losses and racked up debts, said last week that it was in a life threatening profitability and financial situation . on 14 march, one of these creditors property investment fund molsiris which owns the club's stadium holds its agm at which it will discuss the rescue plan. in addition to the interest payment freeze, borussia dortmund also will get short term loans to help pay salaries.
