mixed signals from french economy the french economy picked up speed at the end of 2004 official figures show but still looks set to have fallen short of the government s hopes. despite the apparent shortfall in annual economic growth the good quarterly figures a so called flash estimate mark a continuing trend of improving indicators for the health of the french economy. if confirmed that would be the best quarterly showing since early 2002. it leaves gdp up 2.3 for the full year but short of the 2.5 which the french government had predicted. according to state statistics body insee growth for the three months to december was a seasonally adjusted 0.7 0.8 ahead of the 0.6 forecast. the government is reiterating a 2.5 target for 2005 while the european central bank is making positive noises for the 12 nation eurozone as a whole.
