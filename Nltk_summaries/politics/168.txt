mps murder sentence concern murder sentences should not be reduced automatically simply because of a guilty plea says a new mps report. mps criticised home secretary david blunkett last year for introducing last minute rules allowing reduced sentences for murderers who pleaded guilty. he said by making express provision for murder tariffs in the criminal justice act parliament sent a clear signal that it expects murder to be treated differently to other offences. he said we want to see sentencers advised that in the case of murder reduction in sentence for a guilty plea should not normally be granted in addition to reductions for other mitigating circumstances. amanda s uncle lewis champion told the bbc news website ford did not deserve any credit for his plea saying nothing at all is worth taking five years off a murder sentence.
