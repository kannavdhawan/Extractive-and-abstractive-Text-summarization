mps tout lords replacement plan a group of mps has tried to raise the pressure on tony blair over reform to the house of lords by publishing a detailed blueprint for change. but the all party group including tories ken clarke and sir george young labour s robin cook and tony wright and liberal democrat paul tyler is confident its plan would win support from a large majority . the group says the british public and a clear majority of mps support replacing the lords with a largely elected second chamber. their plan would see the house of lords being renamed the second chamber of parliament and its members would be known as mscps. the cross party group has unveiled a draft bill proposing a smaller second chamber in which 70 of members would be elected.
