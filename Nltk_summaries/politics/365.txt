about 4 300 civil servants will leave london and the south east by the end of march 2005 and there are plans to relocate another 3 500. of those 300 department of health posts will go to yorkshire while hundreds at the department of culture will move to birmingham and newcastle. mark serwotka the leader of the public and commercial services union said in last year s budget we had the day of the long knifes as the chancellor kicked off the crude game of who could cut the most civil service jobs between the government and the tories. at the department for work and pensions 30 000 jobs are to go 560 will be lost by the end of the month at the department of trade and industry and 400 are to go at the inland revenue and customs. whitehall cuts ahead of target thousands of civil service jobs have already been cut or moved out of london as part of a major cost cutting drive. in his budget statement the chancellor said the first 12 500 civil service jobs had been cut on target.
