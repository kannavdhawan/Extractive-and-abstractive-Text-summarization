he passed away at home in east sussex, just 11 days after his wife audrey died aged 91. lord callaghan, who leaves a son and two daughters, was the longest living former british pm in history. he held each of the major offices of chancellor, home secretary, foreign secretary and prime minister during his career and became lord callaghan of cardiff in 1987. chancellor gordon brown said the former pm would be mourned throughout the world . after serving as a junior minister in the attlee government, he became chancellor of the exchequer when labour returned to power in 1964. with sterling under pressure, he resisted devaluation for three years, before being forced into it in 1967. his political career was not without controversy. when he refused to support prime minister harold wilson and dame barbara castle over the latter's trade union manifesto, in place of strife, in 1968, he said i am not going to resign. as foreign secretary in the early 1970s, lord callaghan kept an open mind about the uk's entry into the common market, seeing the advantages of the uk's entry. in the autumn of 1978, before the winter of discontent when trade unions carried out strikes that brought the country to a standstill, lord callaghan refused to hold an early election which may have delivered a labour victory.
